Scottish-Fold

The Scottish Fold is a breed of domestic cat with a natural dominant-gene mutation that affects cartilage throughout the body, causing the ears to "fold", bending forward and down towards the front of the head, which gives the cat what is often described as an "owl-like" appearance. Scottish Fold is also a affectionnate breed thats bond very well with its human caregivers, they also get along with children and other pets at home. Scottish Folds are very cute and kind cats. Scottish Folds are also homebodies and do best when living as indoor cats.

Adopting a Cat/Kitten.

Adopting a Kitten/Cat can be quite hard,
because sometimes your new furry friend might not feel welcomed into their new home,
sometimes they might even completely hate it, and always scratch you.
So in order for that not to happen you have to spend lots of time with your new friend, and train it to love you.
To train the Cat/Kitten you need treats, those furry creatures will do anything for food.
Then once you have a treat, try peting your little friend for some time.
If your furry friend scratches you, DO NOT give the treat to Him/Her.
Otherwise if your Cat/Kitten does not scratch you, you may give him/her the treat.
